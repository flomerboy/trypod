overview

Getting started with trypod is as easy as 3D-printing or buying the printed parts, buying some hardware, and assembling with some glue!

printed parts

The device itself consists mostly 3D printed plastic. First, download the .stl file. Then, print! If you don't have access to a 3D printer, we recommend printing with 3D hubs.You can customize trypod by selecting different types of connectors and arms. All parts print at 30% infill with no supports or rafts necessary.





download parts (.stl)          print with 3D hubs

hardware

In addition to the plastic parts, trypod also requires 9 screws, 8 wingnuts, 4 lengths of aluminum pipe, and 5-minute epoxy. All these parts can easily be found online or at a local hardware store.





internet shopping list (~$50)     or      hardware store shopping list (~$25)

  • wingnuts on amazon. Buy 1 set.
  • hexnuts on inventables. Buy 1 set of 1" and 1 set of 2".
  • aluminum tube from McMaster Carr. Buy 4, or buy one of a longer length if you have a hack saw at home.
  • epoxy from Overstock. Buy 1.
  • 9 1/4-20 wingnuts
  • 9 1/4-20 cap screws with a full thread
  • 4 15" lengths of 1/2" outer diameter, 3/8" inner diameter aluminum tube
  • 5 minute epoxy

assembly

Once you have all the parts above, you're ready to move onto the full instructions! Make sure you have all of the components before moving on.





show instructions

  • step 1: Get all of the parts together.
  • Step 2: Glue the round arm pins into the aluminum tubes. Two of these will have one long and one short pin. Follow the instructions on your epoxy to make sure you get a good bond. It is important that the pins are indexed with each other during this step.
  • Step 3: Put together the base joint.
  • Step 4: Attach the two base arms to the base joint.
  • Step 5: Attach the elbow joint to the base joint as pictured above.
  • Step 6: Attach the two upper arms to the elbow joint.
  • Step 7: Attach the neck joint and neck pin to the upper arms.
  • Step 8: Attach the head to the neck pin.
  • Step 9: Add rubber bands to the joints until it is tight enough to hold up your phone without tightening the bolts, then tighten the bolts slightly.
  • Step 10: Place trypod into it's adapter.
